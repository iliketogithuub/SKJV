The New Testament

Based King James Version (BKJV)

The Supernatural Gospel

What you learned about salvation is true. No one is taking that away. We are adding what was left out. The gospel you heard—Jesus’ blood washing away sins, giving eternal life—is real, biblical, and absolute. It is just not the whole story.

Before we talk about what is missing, let’s be crystal clear on what you already have in the Blood Gospel: Every person born into this world (except Jesus) has sinned. We were all guilty before a holy God. But if you have accepted Christ, that is your old identity. You are a new creation; old things have passed away; look, all things have become new. The penalty for sin is death—not just physical death, but eternal separation from God. But God loved you so much, He sent Jesus to pay the debt. Jesus took your punishment. He shed His blood so you would not have to face death. This is the gospel most churches preach. It is powerful, and it purchased forgiveness, justification, redemption from the curse, and eternal life.

If all Jesus wanted to do was forgive your sin and give you eternal life... Why did He have to suffer so much before He went to the cross? The blood dealt with your sin. The stripes dealt with your sickness.

Seven hundred years before Jesus was born, the prophet Isaiah wrote: "Surely He has borne our sicknesses (choliy) and carried our physical pains (makob)... And by His stripes we are healed." Jesus took your sickness at the whipping post. 1 Peter 2:24 says: "...by whose stripes you were healed." You are not waiting for God to decide whether He wants to heal you. He already made that decision 2,000 years ago when He laid your diseases on Jesus' back.

The Gospel According to John

Chapter 1

1 In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God.
2 This exact Word was in the beginning with God.
3 All things were created through the Word; and apart from the Word, not a single thing was created that exists.
4 Inside the Word was life; and the life was the light of humanity.
5 And the light **shines** inside the darkness; and the darkness has never extinguished the light.

The Gospel According to Mark

Based King James Version (BKJV)

Chapter 11

22 Jesus answered and said, "Have faith in God.
23 For truly I tell you, whoever **says to this mountain**, 'Be lifted up and thrown into the sea,' and **does not doubt within their heart**, but fully **believes that the words spoken with their mouth will happen**—they will have exactly what they have spoken come to pass.
24 Therefore I tell you, whatever things you desire when you pray, **believe that those things have been received**, and those things will manifest.
25 And whenever you **stand to pray**, if you **hold a grudge** against anyone, **forgive them**, so that your Father in heaven may also forgive your sins.
26 But if you do not forgive, neither will your Father in heaven forgive your sins."
Operational Insight

A double-minded heart forces a double-tongued confession. To doubt within the heart is to speak words of alignment one moment and words of destruction the next. Because the mouth prints exactly what is settled in the heart, an individual must eliminate the double-minded loop to ensure the words spoken with the mouth carry absolute execution power.

The Epistle of Paul to Titus

Based King James Version (BKJV)

Chapter 1

1 Paul, a servant of God and an apostle of Jesus Christ, aligned with the faith of God's chosen people and the full recognition of the truth that produces a divine lifestyle;
2 anchored in the absolute certainty of eternal life, which God—who is completely incapable of lying—promised before time even began;
3 and has at the perfect strategic time revealed the spoken word through preaching, which was entrusted to me by the direct command of God our Savior;
4 To Titus, a true child in our shared faith: Grace, mercy, and peace from God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ our Savior.
5 For this exact reason I left you in Crete, so that you would straighten out the things that were left unfinished, and appoint leadership in every city just as I instructed you.
6 Any leader chosen must be beyond reproach, faithful in marriage, having children who walk in faith and are never accused of wild living or rebellion.
7 Because an overseer is managing God’s household, that individual must be blameless—not arrogant, not quick-tempered, not a drunkard, not violent, and not chasing dirty financial gain.
8 Instead, that individual must be hospitable, a lover of what is good, clear-minded, just, holy, and completely self-controlled;
9 holding fast to the trustworthy word exactly as it was taught, so that the leader has the ability to both encourage people with sound doctrine and completely expose and silence those who contradict the truth.
10 Because there are many rebellious people, empty talkers, and deceivers, especially those sticking to old religious rituals like circumcision;
11 the mouths of these deceivers must be stopped, because whole households are being ruined by the teaching of things that should never be taught, all for the sake of dirty financial gain.
12 A local speaker, a prophet from that very culture, even said, “People from Crete are always liars, dangerous beasts, and lazy gluttons.”
13 This testimony is completely true. For this reason, issue a sharp rebuke, so that the believers remain completely healthy and sound in the faith;
14 paying absolutely no attention to religious myths or human commandments that reject the truth.
15 To the clean, all things are clean; but to the defiled and unbelieving, nothing is clean—in fact, both the mind and the conscience of a defiled individual are completely polluted.
16 A public claim is made to know God, but real-world actions completely deny God. Such individuals are detestable, disobedient, and entirely worthless for any good work.
