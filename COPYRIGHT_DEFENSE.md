# Simple King James Version (SKJV) Copyright & Legal Defense Framework

## Executive Summary

The Simple King James Version (SKJV) Bible project is an independent modernization of the public domain 1611 King James Version (KJV). To ensure absolute compliance with international copyright laws and protect the project from copyright infringement claims by modern corporate Bible publishers (such as Thomas Nelson, publishers of the New King James Version / NKJV), this document establishes the legal basis for the SKJV's creation, ownership, and defense.

This document serves as formal legal and technical documentation of **Independent Creation** and explains the specific linguistic mechanisms that distinguish the SKJV from all other modern translations.

---

## 1. Legal Status of the Source Material (1611 KJV)

The underlying text for the SKJV is the original 1611 King James Version (KJV), also known as the Authorized Version. 
- **United States & International Law**: The 1611 KJV text entered the public domain centuries ago. Under U.S. copyright law and the Berne Convention, any work first published prior to 1928 is in the public domain and may be copied, modified, and distributed without permission or royalty.
- **Crown Copyright (UK)**: In the United Kingdom, the Crown maintains a perpetual patent on the Authorized Version. However, this restriction is geographically confined to the UK and does not affect publication or distribution in Australia, the United States, or any other jurisdiction.
- **Conclusion**: The KJV text is 100% free of copyright restrictions for users in the United States and Australia, serving as an open, lawful starting point.

## 2. The SKJV as a Derivative Work (17 U.S.C. § 103)

Under 17 U.S.C. § 103, a translation or adaptation of a public domain work is legally classified as a **"Derivative Work"**. 
- The author of a derivative work obtains a valid copyright covering the **newly contributed original elements** (modernized phrasing, structural adaptations, footnotes, and formatting).
- The underlying public domain text remains free for others to use, but the new translation itself is protected.
- **Originality Threshold**: To qualify for copyright protection, a derivative work must display a "modest quantum of creative expression." Simple corrections of spelling or punctuation do not suffice. The SKJV meets this threshold through a systematic, rule-based linguistic overhaul (e.g., the zero-pronoun rule and the exousia/dunamis translation matrix), establishing robust originality.

## 3. Defense Against Infringement Claims: The Merger Doctrine and "Thin" Copyright

Modern corporate translations like the NKJV are proprietary. If a publisher claims that the SKJV is "substantially similar" to the NKJV, the SKJV defense rests on three established doctrines:

### A. The Merger Doctrine
In copyright law, when an idea or a piece of factual data can only be expressed in a very limited number of ways, the expression "merges" with the idea. Because the historical events and theological statements of scripture are fixed, and because there are only a finite number of ways to translate ancient Greek and Hebrew words accurately, certain verses in the KJV, NKJV, and SKJV will look identical (e.g., John 11:35: *"Jesus wept"*). No publisher can claim a copyright monopoly over these natural translations.

### B. "Thin" Copyright
Because a translation is bound to a historical source text, its copyright is legally "thin." This means it only protects against verbatim, word-for-word duplication of the specific creative changes. It does not prevent another translator from modernizing the KJV independently.

### C. Independent Creation and the "Translation Trail"
To win a copyright lawsuit, a plaintiff must prove that the defendant *copied* their work. If the defendant can prove they created their work independently from a public domain source, the claim of infringement fails. 

The SKJV project establishes an indisputable paper trail of independent creation by:
1. Archiving all AI translation prompts and raw KJV inputs in the `/records` directory.
2. Saving full chat history files showing the step-by-step development of translation rules.
3. Tracking all modifications cryptographically through Git/GitHub commit history.

## 5. U.S. Copyright Office Space 6 Declaration

When filing the official copyright application (Form TX / Standard Application) with the United States Copyright Office, the SKJV will be registered as a derivative work with the following declarations:

- **Space 6a (Material Excluded)**: *"Preexisting public domain 1611 King James Version (KJV) biblical text."*
- **Space 6b (New Material Included)**: *"New modernized English translation, zero-pronoun syntactical adaptations, original editorial revisions, and active-imperative rephrasings."*

This clear declaration bounds the SKJV copyright strictly to its unique, newly contributed modern phrasing, keeping the public domain KJV open to all while legally safeguarding the SKJV.
