How the Advance Works in Practice  
Paying an advance doesn't mean you are paying extra money on top of royalties; it's a pre-payment. It works on an "earn-out" system:

You sign the contract and hand over a $500 advance.

You print your 500 pocket Bibles and start selling them at $25 each.

For the first 200 books you sell ($2.50 royalty × 200 \= $500), you owe the publisher $0. You have already paid them this money via the advance.

On the 201st book sold, you start paying them the $2.50 royalty per unit on your standard reporting schedule (usually quarterly).

The Legal Catch with "Recycling" the NKJV  
If you feed the copyrighted NKJV text into an AI and tell it to "change the words slightly to make a new version," you are creating what copyright law calls a derivative work.

The Rule on Derivative Works: If your new text relies on the structure, rhythm, and specific word choices of a copyrighted book as its base, the original copyright owner still owns the rights to your modified version.

If HarperCollins' legal team saw a "Safe New King James Version" that looked and read 90% like their text with a few words swapped out, they would issue a cease-and-desist faster than you could ship your first batch from Alibaba.
