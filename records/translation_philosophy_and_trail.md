# SKJV Translation Philosophy & Trail Document

This document records the official translation philosophy, linguistic parameters, and specific word mappings for the **Simple King James Version (SKJV)** Bible translation project. It serves as a guide for translators (and AI agents) and forms part of the project's legal record of independent creation.

---

## 1. Core Translation Philosophy

The Simple King James Version (SKJV) is built upon a **Functional Covenant Model** of translation. Unlike traditional modern Bibles that prioritize either formal equivalence (literal word-for-word) or dynamic equivalence (thought-for-thought), the SKJV is designed to read as a **functional operating manual** for the believer.

### A. The Direct-Address Principle
God's Word is treated as a direct broadcast to the reader. Wherever the text describes covenant blessings, identity, or authority, the translation shifts from abstract third-person narratives to direct, personal possession ("my body", "your body", "your Father").

### B. The Zero-Pronoun Rule
To eliminate gender-language disputes, prevent theological smoke screens, and maximize individual application, third-person pronouns (he, she, they, their, his, him) in instruction blocks are systematically replaced with concrete nouns:
- "his heart" -> "the heart" or "their heart"
- "he shall have" -> "that individual will have" or "they will have"
- "them that believe" -> "the believers"

### C. Down-to-Earth Vocabulary (2026 English)
Religious and archaic words that carry centuries of theological dust or have shifted in common usage are replaced with direct, punchy, modern terms:
- "Infirmity" -> "Sickness or Disease"
- "Stripes" -> "Wounds" or "Scourging wounds"
- "Trespasses" -> "Sins"
- "Grievance" -> "A grudge"
- "Lo / Behold" -> "Listen closely / Look"

## 2. Master Translation Parameters & Guidelines

All chapters processed for the SKJV must adhere to the following strict guidelines:

1. **Eliminate All Archaisms**: Completely remove words like *thee, thou, ye, thy, thine, lest, wherefore*, and verbs ending in *-eth* or *-est* (e.g., *saith, walketh, runnest*).
2. **Bold the Functional Directives**: Whenever a verse outlines an explicit command, action step, or state of identity under the Covenant, **bold** the core action words (e.g., **say to this mountain**, **do not doubt**, **believe you receive**).
3. **Insert Bracketed Operational Insights**: After key passages that outline a spiritual mechanism (such as Mark 11:23-24, Luke 10:19, or James 5:14-16), include a brief, italicized `[Operational Insight]` that clarifies the legal and spiritual mechanics of the text.
4. **Distinguish Exousia vs. Dynamis**:
   - `Exousia` (delegated authority/legal standing) must be translated as **delegated authority** or **legal authority**.
   - `Dunamis` (inherent supernatural force/miraculous ability) must be translated as **supernatural power** or **miraculous power**.
5. **Absolute Copyright Safety**: The resulting text must be uniquely phrased and structurally distinct from the NKJV or other copyrighted modern translations, while preserving the exact semantic truth.
6. **Clarify Human Spirit vs. Holy Spirit**: Differentiate the *human spirit* (lowercase) from the *Holy Spirit* (capitalized). The Holy Spirit is treated as a literal spiritual Person (literally a Ghost inhabiting the believer's body as His temple) working in alignment with the believer's human spirit to manifest covenant power. Banish the word "ghost" globally from the translation text to preserve modern readability while reinforcing this literal personal presence.
